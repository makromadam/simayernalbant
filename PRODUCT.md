# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Agencies, startups, and individual recruiters/founders evaluating Simay Ernalbant's web, app, and brand design work. They arrive via a shared link, Behance, or LinkedIn, on desktop or mobile, and scan quickly to judge skill and fit before deciding whether contacting her is worth their time. They are not browsing for inspiration — they are vetting a potential hire or collaborator.

## Product Purpose

A personal portfolio site for Simay Ernalbant that demonstrates design capability convincingly enough to convert a visitor into outreach — via WhatsApp, LinkedIn, or Behance. The work shown (App Design, Web Design, Education & Past) and the craft of the site itself are both part of the pitch.

## Brand Personality

Cinematic, premium/elevated, bold/confident. The voice is dramatic but exact: glowing 3D visuals (head/hand/brain video assets) are treated with technical precision — mix-blend-mode compositing, an orbital diagram, deliberate motion timing — rather than playful or whimsical. Confidence comes from restraint and precision, not from noise.

## Anti-references

No specific sites named. Default to avoiding generic AI-template portfolio tropes — tiny uppercase eyebrows on every section, gradient text, glassmorphism-as-default, identical icon-card grids, the hero-metric template — while preserving the established reference.png-driven cinematic orange/amber direction already implemented. Numbered section markers (02/03/04) are already in use and are acceptable here because they trace a real, ordered path through the portfolio (Expertise → Web → Education), not decorative scaffolding — but this is worth revisiting in a future critique/audit.

## Design Principles

- Let the work be the proof — video assets and project specifics carry more credibility than claims of skill.
- Cinematic restraint — drama comes from light, glow, and motion on a small number of focal elements, not decoration everywhere.
- One clear path to contact — every section should ultimately point toward WhatsApp, LinkedIn, or Behance, never bury the CTA.
- Technical precision as a craft signal — exact video compositing, orbit-diagram geometry, and motion timing double as proof of the designer's own skill.
- Multilingual without compromise — EN/TR/FR parity is part of the brand's professionalism, not an afterthought.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA minimum: body text ≥4.5:1 contrast, large text ≥3:1, a `prefers-reduced-motion` alternative for scroll-reveal/glow/ember animations, and keyboard-navigable primary nav and mobile menu.
